How To Play

Minesweeper can seem a complex game at first glance, but it really is simpler than it seems.
The rules are few and simple:

  • Some cells contain bombs
  • Every safe cell contains the number of surrounding bombs
    • The count also considers cells neighboring along the diagonals
  • Revealing a cell with no neighboring bombs will automatically reveal all surrounding cells
    • This behaviour is then replicated on the neighboring cells, sometimes revealing large portions of the map
  • Revealing a bomb makes you lose the game
    • You can place flags on cells that you suspect may hide a bomb, to keep track of them
  • You win the game by revealing all safe cells
